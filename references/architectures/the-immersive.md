# The Immersive

**Best for:** wedding-venue, luxury detailing, premium restaurant
**Personality:** cinematic, atmospheric, sensory, aspirational
**Tier:** 4
**Section count:** 7-8
**Animation budget:** 6+ scroll-driven effects (highest budget)

## Emotional Arc
- **Arrival:** Wonder — "This is an experience, not a website"
- **Building:** Immersion — "I can almost feel what it's like to be there"
- **Decision:** Desire — "I have to experience this in person"

## Rhythm Pattern
HERO → HERO → w → s → HERO → w → s → w

## Required Sections
- Hero (fullscreen — video background, parallax, or Ken Burns)
- Gallery fullscreen (scroll-driven reveal, each image nearly viewport-sized)
- Services / experiences (atmospheric descriptions, not bullet lists)
- About (philosophy/story, scroll-triggered)
- Reviews (cinematic presentation — large type, dramatic)
- CTA break (invitation to visit / book / experience)
- Contact (elegant form or booking integration)
- Footer (atmospheric, brand-consistent)

## Optional Sections
- Virtual tour / walkthrough
- Awards / press in editorial format
- Events calendar
- Chef's table / VIP packages
- Seasonal highlights
- Behind-the-scenes scroll story

## Signature Characteristic
Cinematic feel with the highest motion budget in the system — scroll-driven parallax, reveal animations, and full-viewport sections create an experience that feels more like a film trailer than a website. Every scroll tick triggers something beautiful.

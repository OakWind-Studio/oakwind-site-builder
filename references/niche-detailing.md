# Niche: Auto Detailing

Covers auto detailing, car detailing, ceramic coating, paint protection film (PPF), paint correction, interior detailing, mobile detailing, and specialty vehicle care.

---

## Required Page Elements

- **Before/after comparison sliders** — dramatic transformation is the #1 conversion tool
- **Service packages with clear pricing tiers** — detailing is highly package-driven
- **Photo gallery** showcasing completed work (this niche is 90% visual)
- **Phone number** — click-to-call, always visible
- **Google rating and review count** — first viewport
- **"Book Now" or scheduling CTA** — persistent
- **Vehicle types served** (sedans, SUVs, trucks, boats, RVs if applicable)
- **Product/brand mentions** — Ceramic Pro, XPEL, Gtechniq signal quality

## Trust Signals (priority order)

1. Before/after gallery (the portfolio IS the pitch)
2. Google rating + review count
3. Certification badges (IDA, Ceramic Pro certified, XPEL installer)
4. Product brand partnerships
5. Years in business
6. Number of vehicles detailed
7. Owner name and photo
8. Specific guarantees ("2-year ceramic coating warranty")

## Section Priority Order

1. Hero (stunning car photo + name + tagline + Book Now)
2. Trust strip (rating, certifications, vehicles completed)
3. Service packages (pricing tiers — most important section)
4. Before/after gallery
5. About (owner story, training, passion)
6. Reviews (vehicle-specific)
7. Booking / contact
8. Footer

## Aesthetic Direction

Auto detailing sites should feel **premium, dramatic, and showcase-driven**. Think: luxury car photography, performance brand aesthetics. The cars are the stars — the design exists to frame them.

### Color Approach
- Dark backgrounds: black, charcoal, deep slate
- Metallic/chrome accents: silver, gunmetal, platinum
- Single bold accent: electric blue, amber, red
- Gradient overlays on dark backgrounds for depth
- Avoid: pastel, earthy, or overly bright palettes

### Typography
- Condensed or bold sans-serif: aggressive, automotive feel
- Uppercase headlines with wide letter-spacing
- Thin body text on dark backgrounds (high contrast)
- Never: script, serif, or playful fonts

### Photography
- Dramatic lighting: studio-quality shots with reflections
- Close-ups of paint correction, ceramic beading water
- Black backgrounds for vehicle shots
- Detail shots: swirl marks → mirror finish
- Action: polishing, applying coating, steaming interior

## Service Package Display Pattern

Detailing is package-driven. Display tiers clearly:

### Three-Tier Standard
1. **Basic / Maintenance** — exterior wash, interior vacuum, windows
2. **Premium / Full Detail** — everything above + clay bar, polish, interior deep clean
3. **Ultimate / Ceramic Package** — everything above + paint correction + ceramic coating

Each tier should show:
- Package name (branded: "The Works", "Showroom", "Concours")
- Price (or "Starting at $X")
- What's included (bulleted list)
- Time estimate
- Clear "Book Now" CTA per tier

### Display Patterns
- Side-by-side cards with the middle tier highlighted ("Most Popular")
- Vertical stack on mobile (no horizontal scroll for pricing)
- Feature comparison grid for 3+ tiers

---

## Before/After Slider (Enhanced for Detailing)

Same slider mechanic as pressure washing, but with detailing-specific touches:
- Dark background behind the slider container
- Subtle glow or reflection effect on the "after" side
- Labels: "BEFORE" (muted) and "AFTER" (accented)
- Include the service performed: "Paint Correction + Ceramic Coating"
- Mobile: full-width, swipe-friendly

## Copy Patterns That Work

- "Your car deserves better than a car wash"
- "Obsessive attention to detail — every surface, every time"
- "Certified [Product] installer — backed by a [X]-year warranty"
- "[X]+ vehicles transformed"
- "The [city] detail shop car enthusiasts trust"
- "From daily drivers to exotics — we treat every vehicle like our own"
- "See the difference. Feel the difference."

## Anti-Patterns

- Light/white backgrounds — makes car photos look flat, loses the premium feel
- Stock car photos — customers want to see YOUR work
- No pricing — detailing customers expect transparent package pricing
- Cluttered service lists without packages — organize into clear tiers
- Generic "auto care" language — lean into the craft and precision of detailing
- Missing before/after — the transformation IS the marketing

# Niche: Auto Repair

Covers auto repair shops, mechanics, tire shops, oil change services, transmission specialists, brake shops, and general automotive service.

---

## Required Page Elements

- **Customer reviews on every page** — 85% of customers research shops online before choosing
- **ASE / NAPA / AAA certification badges** prominently displayed
- **Before/after repair photos** (when available)
- **Transparent pricing** or "free estimate" messaging
- **Sticky appointment scheduling CTA** — phone number always visible
- **Service-specific pages/sections** for SEO (brakes, oil change, transmission, tires, AC, etc.)
- **Warranty/guarantee information** — signals confidence
- **Hours of operation** — must include Saturday if applicable

## Trust Signals (priority order)

1. Google rating + review count (first viewport)
2. Years in business / "family-owned since XXXX"
3. ASE Certified / NAPA AutoCare / AAA Approved badges
4. Owner name and photo — personal accountability
5. "Licensed & Insured" badge
6. Specific service warranties ("12-month/12,000-mile warranty")
7. Real shop photos (not stock) — bay, equipment, team
8. Service area specificity ("Serving Fort Worth & Tarrant County")

## Section Priority Order

1. Hero (shop photo + name + rating + phone CTA)
2. Trust strip (rating, years, certifications, warranty)
3. Services (organized by category with brief descriptions)
4. About (owner story, team, heritage narrative)
5. Reviews (minimum 3, with service-specific details)
6. Location + hours + map
7. Contact / appointment request
8. Footer with OakWind credit

## Copy Patterns That Work

- "Honest work, fair prices — since {year}"
- "Your car is in good hands" (with owner photo)
- "We fix it right the first time — guaranteed"
- "ASE-certified technicians you can trust"
- "No surprise fees. No upselling. Just quality repairs."
- "[Owner name] and his team have been keeping [city] on the road for {years} years"

## Anti-Patterns

- Stock photos of generic mechanics — always use real shop photos or Unsplash with specific searches
- Blue-and-white with no personality — add warmth or industrial edge
- Listing services without descriptions — each service needs at least one line of why-it-matters
- Hiding the phone number — it should be impossible to miss
- Overly technical jargon — customers want trust, not specs

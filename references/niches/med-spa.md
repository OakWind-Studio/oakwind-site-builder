# Niche: Med Spa / Aesthetic Clinic

Covers medical spas, aesthetic clinics, Botox/filler providers, laser treatment centers, body contouring studios, wellness/IV therapy clinics, anti-aging centers, and cosmetic treatment providers.

Med spas are one of the highest-value niches for OakWind. These are not small skin care studios — premium aesthetic clinics generate $50K-$150K/month in revenue with board-certified providers, six-figure equipment investments, and affluent clientele who expect luxury at every touchpoint. The website is the patient's first impression of the practice, and in a niche where the product IS appearance, a dated or generic site is a dealbreaker. Patients researching Botox, fillers, or laser treatments will visit 3-5 provider websites before booking — the one that looks and feels the most premium wins.

---

## Why This Niche Pays Premium

- A single new patient is worth $1,200-$3,000 in year-one revenue (Botox alone is $400-$600 per session, 3-4x/year). One patient covers the website cost.
- Patients judge the quality of aesthetic work by the quality of the website. A cheap-looking site signals cheap-looking results. Providers understand this viscerally.
- Med spas compete directly with other local providers for the same affluent clientele. A premium website is a competitive weapon shown on iPads during consultations and shared on social media.
- This niche supports Tier 3-4 pricing ($3.5K-$10K) because providers already spend $5K-$15K/month on marketing. A website that converts even marginally better pays for itself in weeks.
- Providers are brand-conscious — many have backgrounds in aesthetics and design. They notice and appreciate premium details like smooth scrolling, orchestrated animations, and editorial photography layouts.

---

## Required Page Elements

- **Before/after galleries** — the single most powerful conversion element in this niche. Use a lightbox gallery, not inline sliders. Patients want to zoom in, compare, and study results. Include "results may vary" disclaimer.
- **Persistent "Book Now" button** — always visible, never more than one scroll away. Use a sticky header CTA or a floating button on mobile. Link to online booking (Vagaro, Boulevard, Jane, or custom form).
- **Provider bios with credentials** — MD, DO, NP, RN, PA displayed prominently with professional headshots. Board certifications, training institutions, years of experience, and areas of specialization. These are not buried in an "About" page — they appear in the top 3 sections.
- **Treatment/service showcase** — organized by category (injectables, laser, body, skin, wellness). Each treatment gets a brief description, what to expect, and a "Book Consultation" CTA. Use a horizontal scroll showcase or card grid.
- **Pricing transparency** — at minimum, "starting at" prices or "complimentary consultation." Affluent patients are not price-sensitive, but they dislike feeling ambushed. Transparency signals confidence.
- **HIPAA compliance note** on any form that collects health information
- **Google/RealSelf rating and review count** — social proof is critical for elective procedures. Display prominently in the trust strip.
- **"New Patient" special or consultation offer** — lowers barrier to first visit. "Complimentary consultation" is more effective than discounts for premium positioning.
- **Availability / next-appointment CTA** — "Next Available Appointment: [date]" or "Same-Week Appointments Available" creates urgency without pressure. Works especially well in the hero or trust strip.

## Trust Signals (priority order)

1. Provider credentials, board certifications, and professional headshots
2. Before/after results gallery with patient consent
3. Google/RealSelf rating + review count (e.g., "4.9 stars from 340+ reviews")
4. Years of experience / number of procedures performed
5. Brand partnerships and device certifications (Allergan Diamond, Galderma, CoolSculpting, Sciton)
6. Professional, spa-like facility photos
7. Treatment-specific testimonials with outcomes
8. "Complimentary consultation" offer
9. Awards and media features

## Section Priority Order

1. Hero (aspirational beauty imagery + practice name + tagline + Book Now)
2. Trust strip (credentials, rating, years, "board-certified," next available appointment)
3. Treatment showcase (organized by category, horizontal scroll or elegant grid)
4. Before/after gallery (lightbox with zoom)
5. About / providers (bios, credentials, philosophy, professional headshots)
6. Reviews (treatment-specific testimonials with outcomes)
7. Special offers / new patient consultation
8. Location + hours + booking
9. Footer

## Aesthetic Direction

The core challenge: blend **luxury spa feeling** with **medical credibility**. Too spa-like feels unserious for medical procedures. Too clinical feels cold and unwelcoming. The sweet spot is a luxury hotel or high-end dermatology office: elegant, calming, and unmistakably professional. Every visual choice should communicate: "This is where discerning people come for exceptional results."

### Color Approach

**Default to soft, warm palettes.** Med spas sell transformation and confidence — the palette should feel aspirational and soothing, never sterile. Think luxury skincare packaging (La Mer, SkinCeuticals, Augustinus Bader) rather than a doctor's office.

**Warm luxe (DEFAULT):** Blush, champagne, warm cream with rose gold or muted gold accents. Aspirational femininity without being gendered. This is the safest choice for most med spas.

**Sage & cream:** Soft sage, ivory, warm white with deep green and gold accents. Feels natural, clean, wellness-forward. Best for practices emphasizing holistic or natural approaches.

**Lavender & white:** Cool lavender tints, clean white, soft purple with gold. Refined, modern, slightly more clinical. Best for dermatology-adjacent practices.

**Luxury dark (only for anti-aging/luxury sub-niche):** Deep warm charcoal (never pure black) with champagne or brass accents. Only when the practice specifically positions as ultra-luxury or targets 45+ affluent clients.

Avoid: Bright/saturated colors, neon, primary colors, red (triggers medical anxiety), clinical blue (feels like a hospital), pure white backgrounds without warmth.

White space is premium — use it generously. Let images and typography breathe.

### Typography

- Elegant serif for display headlines: signals refinement, taste, and trust
- Clean sans-serif for body: signals modern medicine and precision
- The practice NAME should be a prominent typographic element — not as large as a realtor's name, but clearly the brand anchor
- Generous letter-spacing on uppercase labels (tracking-wider or tracking-widest)
- Never: playful, handwritten, overly bold, or clinical/technical fonts

## Sub-Niche Variations

### Medical Aesthetics (Botox / Fillers / Injectables)

The most common and highest-revenue sub-niche. These practices live and die by their injector's reputation.

- Provider credentials front and center — patients choose the injector, not the practice
- Before/after gallery is THE conversion driver. Organize by treatment area (lips, jawline, under-eye, forehead)
- "Natural-looking results" messaging — patients fear looking overdone
- Horizontal scroll treatment showcase: Botox, Dysport, Juvederm, Restylane, Sculptra, Kybella, etc.
- Warm, soft palette (Palette A default). Never clinical.
- CTA: "Book Your Complimentary Consultation" or "Schedule with [Provider Name]"
- Copy tone: confident expertise, never salesy. "Artistry meets precision."

### Laser Clinic

Technology-forward practices with significant equipment investments ($100K-$500K in devices).

- Device/brand partnerships displayed prominently (Sciton, Cynosure, Cutera, Candela)
- Treatment pages organized by concern: hair removal, skin resurfacing, pigmentation, vascular, tattoo removal
- Slightly more modern/clean aesthetic — the technology IS the differentiator
- Before/after results are essential — laser results are dramatic and visible
- Include "How it works" sections with clear expectations (sessions needed, downtime, results timeline)
- Sage & cream palette works well — clean, precise, technology-forward
- CTA: "Book a Skin Assessment" or "Schedule Your Treatment Plan"

### Body Contouring

CoolSculpting, Emsculpt, truSculpt, Morpheus8 Body, liposuction alternatives.

- Results-driven layout — before/after is everything in this sub-niche
- Stats-heavy trust signals: "2,000+ body contouring treatments performed"
- Treatment comparison sections: help patients understand which technology is right for them
- Gender-inclusive imagery and copy — body contouring serves all genders
- Slightly bolder aesthetic — more contrast, more confidence in the design
- CTA: "Book Your Body Assessment" or "Get Your Custom Treatment Plan"
- Copy tone: empowering, body-positive, results-focused. Never shame-based.

### Wellness / IV Therapy

IV drip bars, vitamin therapy, hormone optimization, regenerative medicine.

- Wellness-forward, natural-feeling design — sage, earth tones, organic shapes
- Menu-style service presentation (like a restaurant menu): drip names, ingredients, benefits, pricing
- Lifestyle/aspirational imagery rather than clinical — active people, energy, vitality
- Lighter, more approachable feel than surgical med spas
- Membership/package pricing displayed clearly — recurring revenue model
- CTA: "Book Your Drip" or "Join Our Wellness Membership"
- Copy tone: energetic, modern wellness. "Optimize. Restore. Thrive."

### Anti-Aging Center

Premium practices targeting 40-65+ affluent patients. Regenerative aesthetics, PRP, microneedling, thread lifts, combination therapies.

- **This is the luxury dark sub-niche.** Deep charcoal with champagne/gold accents.
- Editorial, magazine-quality layout — think Vogue or Harper's Bazaar health section
- Provider credentials are paramount — these patients want MDs and DOs, not just NPs
- Lenis smooth scroll, parallax, full-width imagery — the full premium pattern stack
- Language: "Age gracefully," "Regenerative aesthetics," "Comprehensive rejuvenation"
- Longer-form content — these patients research extensively before committing
- CTA: "Request a Private Consultation" or "Schedule with Dr. [Name]"
- Copy tone: sophisticated, authoritative, never trendy. "The science of timeless beauty."

## Treatment Showcase Patterns

### Horizontal Scroll Treatment Cards
A row of treatment category cards that scrolls horizontally. Each card shows: treatment image, category name (Injectables, Laser, Body, Skin), a one-line description, and "Learn More" link. Use CSS `overflow-x-auto` with snap points or `embla-carousel-react`. Cards should be large enough to feel premium: `w-[85vw] md:w-[45vw] lg:w-[35vw]`.

### Before/After Gallery with Lightbox
A grid of before/after thumbnail pairs. On click, opens a full-screen lightbox showing the pair side by side with treatment details (procedure, provider, timeline). Include "results may vary" in the lightbox. Use a masonry or uniform grid. Minimum 6 pairs for a convincing gallery.

### Provider Bio Cards
Full-width or split-layout provider sections. Each provider gets: professional headshot (not a thumbnail — large and high-quality), name with credentials (e.g., "Sarah Chen, MD, FAAD"), board certifications listed, specializations, a brief personal statement, and a "Book with Dr. Chen" CTA. Credentials should be visually prominent — use the accent color or a badge-style treatment.

### Treatment Journey (Sticky Scroll)
Use the sticky scroll narrative pattern for "Your Treatment Journey" or "What to Expect." Left side pins a headline and aspirational image. Right side scrolls through steps: Consultation, Custom Treatment Plan, Your Procedure, Recovery & Aftercare, Your Results. Creates an immersive, premium feel that reduces anxiety about the process.

## Booking & Availability Patterns

### Sticky Header CTA
The "Book Now" button lives in the sticky nav and is always one tap away. On mobile, it can be a floating button at the bottom of the viewport. Use the accent color so it stands out without clashing.

### Availability Signal in Trust Strip
Add "Next Available: Thursday, March 19" or "Same-Week Appointments" to the trust strip. Creates urgency without pressure and signals that the practice is active and accessible.

### Treatment Page CTAs
Every treatment page or card should end with a specific booking CTA: "Book Your [Treatment] Consultation" rather than generic "Contact Us." Link to the online booking system with the treatment pre-selected when possible.

### Consultation Offer Section
A dedicated full-width section (use a contrasting background color) with: headline ("Your Transformation Starts Here"), brief description of what the consultation includes, what to bring/expect, and a prominent booking button. This section replaces the generic "Contact" section in premium builds.

## Anti-Patterns

- Syringes, needles, or clinical equipment as hero imagery — triggers anxiety
- Stock photos of models with perfect skin and no connection to the practice
- Generic "Contact Us" CTA — be specific: "Book Your Consultation" or "Schedule with Dr. [Name]"
- Hiding provider credentials below the fold — they are THE trust signal
- Listing every treatment on the homepage without hierarchy — choose 4-6 categories and let patients drill down
- Using clinical/medical language in patient-facing copy — translate everything to benefit-focused language
- Discount-heavy messaging ("50% off Botox!") — undermines premium positioning. Use "complimentary consultation" instead
- Small, low-quality before/after photos — these are the most scrutinized images on the site. They must be large, well-lit, and consistent
- Too many colors or gradients — luxury is restraint. One accent color, generous white space
- Comic Sans, Papyrus, or any novelty font — unfortunately common in this niche

## Compliance Notes

- All before/after photos require patient consent (assume consent for mockup, note for client)
- HIPAA compliance notice required on any health information form
- Treatment claims must be factual — avoid superlatives like "best" or "guaranteed results"
- Include "results may vary" disclaimer near before/after images
- FDA-cleared vs. FDA-approved — use correct terminology for devices
- Scope of practice varies by state — do not imply services beyond the provider's license
- Testimonials should be real or clearly marked as examples in mockups

## Same-Niche Rotation System

Build 10 med spa sites and they should all look different. Pick the variant that matches the practice personality, and rotate so consecutive same-niche builds use different variants.

| | **Variant A: Blush Luxury / Soft Editorial** | **Variant B: Dark Sophisticated / Clinical Luxury** | **Variant C: Bright Modern / Wellness-Forward** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero | Full-screen aspirational beauty image with soft blush overlay, practice name in elegant serif, "Book Your Consultation" in rose gold accent | Dark warm charcoal hero with champagne gold typography, dramatic provider portrait on split-screen, editorial magazine layout | Bright airy hero with generous whitespace, sage and cream tones, lifestyle wellness imagery, modern asymmetric text placement |
| Palette | Palette A (Blush & Gold — warm white + blush cream + rose gold + sage) or Blush Cream from color-palettes.md | Palette D (Luxury Dark — warm charcoal + champagne gold + muted gold) or Midnight Velvet | Palette B (Sage & Cream — soft cream + sage tint + deep sage + gold) or Sage Linen |
| Texture | Soft blush gradient washes between sections, subtle warm grain on hero, rose-gold thin divider lines | Dark textured sections with subtle noise grain, champagne metallic accents on borders and dividers, dramatic contrast between dark and light sections | None — ultra-clean with organic rounded shapes as section dividers, soft sage tinted alternating sections |
| Cards | Elegant cream cards with thin rose-gold border, serif treatment names, generous whitespace, soft shadow | Dark glass cards with champagne border accent, treatment names in gold, dramatic before/after pairs on dark backgrounds | Rounded sage-tinted cards with clean sans typography, treatment icons in sage, airy spacing |
| CTA style | Rose gold pill button with elegant serif text ("Begin Your Transformation") | Champagne gold button on dark background with subtle glow, authoritative copy ("Request a Private Consultation") | Sage green rounded button with friendly modern copy ("Book Your Visit"), clean and inviting |
| Special | Horizontal scroll treatment showcase with editorial photography, provider bio as magazine-style feature spread | Full-screen before/after gallery with dark lightbox, luxury brand partner logos on dark strip, animated credential badges | Wellness journey sticky-scroll narrative, treatment category cards with hover-reveal descriptions, membership/package pricing display |
| Font mood | Soft luxury serif (Cormorant Garamond or Playfair Display + DM Sans) | Bold editorial serif on dark (Bodoni Moda or Noto Serif Display + Outfit) | Modern clean serif (DM Serif Display or Lora + Nunito Sans) |

**How to pick:** Match variant to practice personality. A blush-and-beauty focused injectable/filler practice with feminine branding → Variant A. An ultra-luxury anti-aging center or provider targeting affluent 40-65+ clients → Variant B. A wellness-forward practice emphasizing IV therapy, holistic treatments, or natural aesthetics → Variant C. Document which variant in App.jsx header comment.

# Niche: Real Estate Agent / Realtor

Covers individual real estate agents, broker teams, boutique brokerages, luxury realtors, and real estate investor-facing agents.

Real estate is one of the highest-value niches for OakWind. A great website isn't optional for agents competing at the $500K+ listing level — it's the difference between getting the listing appointment and getting passed over. The agent's website is their storefront, their resume, and their pitch deck rolled into one.

---

## Why This Niche Pays Premium

- Agents earn 2.5-3% commission on $400K+ homes. One listing covers the website cost many times over.
- The website is shown to prospective sellers during listing presentations on iPads — exactly our demo scenario.
- Agents compete on perceived professionalism and market presence. A premium website is a competitive weapon.
- This niche supports Tier 3-4 pricing ($3.5K-$10K) because ROI is immediately obvious to the client.

---

## Required Page Elements

- **Agent hero with professional headshot** — this is a personal brand business. The agent's face, name, and tagline must dominate the first viewport. Not a stock photo. If no headshot is provided, use a gradient hero with the agent's name oversized.
- **Market stats / social proof numbers** — homes sold, total volume ($47M+), average days on market, years of experience. These are the equivalent of a restaurant's star rating.
- **Property showcase** — featured listings or recent sales in a visually rich grid or horizontal scroll. Even as placeholders, this section must feel like a luxury property portal.
- **Testimonials from clients** — specific outcomes: "Sold our home in 5 days, $15K above asking." Not vague praise.
- **Neighborhood/area expertise** — which neighborhoods or cities they specialize in. Map or area cards.
- **"What Sets Me Apart" / process section** — what working with this agent looks like, step by step.
- **CTA: "Schedule a Consultation" or "Get a Home Valuation"** — never "Contact Us" (too generic). The CTA should match what agents actually want prospects to do.
- **Designations and affiliations** — NAR, local association, GRI, CRS, ABR, Luxury Collection, etc.
- **Phone, email, social links** — agents live on their phones. Click-to-call is critical.

## Trust Signals (priority order)

1. Transaction volume and homes sold ("$47M+ in sales" / "200+ families served")
2. Professional headshot — confident, approachable, well-lit
3. Specific client testimonials with outcomes
4. Years in the market / local expertise
5. Designations and certifications
6. Brokerage affiliation (Keller Williams, Compass, Sotheby's, eXp, etc.)
7. Awards (Top Producer, Circle of Excellence, etc.)
8. Community involvement
9. Social media presence / follower count

## Section Priority Order

1. Hero (agent name, headshot/gradient, tagline, market stats, CTA)
2. Trust strip (homes sold, volume, years, avg days on market)
3. About / personal story (why real estate, local roots, philosophy)
4. Property showcase (featured listings or recent sales)
5. Neighborhoods / areas of expertise
6. Process / "What to Expect"
7. Testimonials (outcome-focused)
8. Contact / consultation booking
9. Footer

## Aesthetic Direction

Real estate agent sites should project **success, sophistication, and local authority**. Think luxury hotel lobby — not flashy, but unmistakably premium. Every pixel should communicate: "This person is successful, connected, and will get your home sold."

### Color Approach

**Default to light/warm palettes.** Real estate is aspirational — buyers and sellers want to feel welcomed, not intimidated. Light backgrounds with warm accents feel like flipping through an architectural magazine. Dark mode is only appropriate for the luxury $1M+ sub-niche, and even then, warm dark (charcoal, not pure black) with plenty of warm white text.

**Warm sophisticated (DEFAULT):** Cream, taupe, warm grey with copper or muted gold. Approachable luxury. This is the safest choice for most agents.

**Modern neutral:** White/cream base with black text and a single refined accent (sage, terracotta, deep blue). Clean and contemporary.

**Luxury dark (only for luxury sub-niche):** Deep navy or warm charcoal (never pure black) with brass/gold accents. Only when the agent specifically positions as ultra-luxury.

Avoid: Bright primary colors, real estate cliches (house icons, key symbols), generic blue, pure black backgrounds.

### Typography

- Elegant serif for display: signals taste and refinement
- Clean sans for body: modern professionalism
- The agent's NAME should be the biggest typographic element on the page — bigger than any headline
- Generous letter-spacing on uppercase labels

### Photography — Images Are Non-Negotiable

Real estate sites without images feel incomplete. For iPad demos, always include placeholder images even if the agent hasn't provided their own yet. Use `picsum.photos` with descriptive seeds:

- **Agent headshot:** `https://picsum.photos/seed/realtor-portrait/600/800` — or use the split-screen hero with a gradient + decorative initials if you want to signal "your photo goes here" elegantly
- **Hero background:** `https://picsum.photos/seed/luxury-home-exterior/1600/900`
- **Property listings (use different seeds):**
  - `https://picsum.photos/seed/modern-house-1/800/600`
  - `https://picsum.photos/seed/home-interior-kitchen/800/600`
  - `https://picsum.photos/seed/suburban-house-2/800/600`
  - `https://picsum.photos/seed/home-living-room/800/600`
- **Neighborhoods:** `https://picsum.photos/seed/downtown-fort-worth/800/500`
- Minimum 6 images per realtor site

Avoid: stock photos of house keys, handshakes, or generic suburbs. When the agent provides their own photos later, these placeholders get swapped out.

## Sub-Niche Variation

### Luxury Real Estate ($1M+)
- Dark themes with metallic accents (gold, champagne, platinum)
- Lenis smooth scroll, parallax, horizontal property showcase
- Magazine-quality editorial layout
- White-glove language: "Private showings," "Exclusive listings"
- Reference luxury brands aesthetically: Four Seasons, Sotheby's

### First-Time Buyer Focused
- Warmer, more approachable palette
- More educational content: "How to Buy Your First Home" process section
- Community/neighborhood focus
- Friendly but professional — not intimidating
- CTA: "Schedule a Free Buyer Consultation"

### Team / Small Brokerage
- Showcase multiple agents with individual cards
- Office location and team photo
- Combined stats (team volume, homes sold)
- More corporate/structured layout

### Investor-Focused Agent
- Data-heavy: market trends, cap rates, ROI projections
- Darker, more analytical aesthetic
- CTA: "Get Investment Property Alerts"
- Portfolio/case study approach to listings

## Property Showcase Patterns

### Horizontal Scroll Gallery
A row of property cards that scrolls horizontally within the vertical page. Each card shows: photo, address, price, bed/bath/sqft. Use `embla-carousel-react` or CSS `overflow-x-auto` with snap points.

### Bento Property Grid
Mixed-size cards: one large featured listing spanning 2 columns, 2-4 smaller recent sales below. The featured listing gets a gradient overlay with price and key stats.

### Stats-First Cards
Property cards where the numbers dominate: `$425,000` in oversized type, address below, small photo. Emphasizes the agent's market activity over individual properties.

## Copy Patterns That Work

- "Your home deserves an agent who treats it like their own"
- "$47M+ in closed transactions — and counting"
- "Fort Worth's most trusted name in real estate"
- "From first showing to closing day, [Name] makes it seamless"
- "Not just an agent. Your strategic partner in real estate."
- "Selling [city] homes faster, for more. Here's how."
- "[X] homes sold. [X] happy families. One commitment: yours."
- "Schedule a no-obligation consultation — let's talk about your goals"

## Anti-Patterns

- House key icons, "Home Sweet Home," or clip-art real estate imagery — screams cheap
- Hiding the agent behind the brokerage brand — the agent IS the brand
- Generic "Contact Us" CTA — be specific: "Get a Home Valuation" or "Schedule a Consultation"
- IDX search as the hero — that's for portals like Zillow, not personal agent sites
- Stock photos of couples holding keys — everyone uses these, they signal nothing
- Too many colors — luxury is restraint. One accent color max.

## Same-Niche Rotation System

Build 10 realtor sites and they should all look different. Pick the variant that matches the agent's personality, and rotate so consecutive same-niche builds use different variants.

| | **Variant A: Dark Luxury / Split-Screen Agent** | **Variant B: Light Editorial / Property-Focused** | **Variant C: Modern Minimal / Data-Driven** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero | Dark warm charcoal hero with split-screen — agent professional headshot on one side, name in oversized gold display type + market stats + CTA on the other, brass/gold accents | Full-bleed luxury property photo hero with light overlay, agent name and tagline in elegant serif, warm cream/copper palette, editorial magazine feel | Clean white hero with bold oversized agent name as the dominant element, minimal property photo accent, key stats (homes sold, volume) animated on load |
| Palette | Palette C (Luxury Dark — warm charcoal + brass gold + muted gold) or Navy Brass from color-palettes.md | Palette A (Warm Sophisticated — cream + warm linen + copper) or Ink & Gold | Palette B (Modern Neutral — warm white + light taupe + black + sage) or Pearl |
| Texture | Dark sections with subtle noise grain, gold metallic divider lines, dramatic light-to-dark section transitions | Warm linen texture on alternating sections, thin copper rules between sections, soft parallax on property images | None — ultra-clean with strong grid structure, sharp lines, bold typographic hierarchy as the only visual texture |
| Cards | Dark glass property cards with gold price overlay, elegant stats layout (bed/bath/sqft in spaced small caps) | Warm cream property cards with copper accent border, editorial photo treatment with soft shadow, neighborhood labels | Flat white cards with bold price in oversized type, minimal photo, stats-forward layout, thin border on hover |
| CTA style | Gold button on dark background with subtle shimmer, "Schedule a Private Consultation" | Warm copper pill button with serif text, "Get Your Home Valuation" | Minimal black button with clean arrow icon, direct copy ("Let's Talk") |
| Special | Cinematic property horizontal scroll on dark rail with gold price tags, animated market volume counter | Neighborhood spotlight sections with editorial photography, testimonials as pull-quote typography features | Interactive market data section (homes sold, avg days on market, price trends), property filter/sort showcase |
| Font mood | Luxury serif on dark (Playfair Display or Bodoni Moda + DM Sans) | Warm editorial serif (Cormorant Garamond or Lora + Outfit) | Bold modern sans with serif accent (Montserrat or DM Serif Display + Source Sans 3) |

**How to pick:** Match variant to agent personality. A luxury agent targeting $1M+ listings who wants maximum prestige → Variant A. A relationship-driven agent with warm personal brand and strong local roots → Variant B. A data-savvy, modern agent who leads with market expertise and volume → Variant C. Document which variant in App.jsx header comment.
- Small agent photo buried in the About section — it should be in the hero or near-hero

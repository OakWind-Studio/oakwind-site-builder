# Curated Color Palettes

Stop picking colors from abstract descriptions. These are tested, production-ready palettes that work beautifully on screens. Each palette includes all six CSS variables the build system needs, plus guidance on which niches and moods they serve.

The palette you choose sets the emotional tone of the entire site. It's the single biggest quality lever you have — a beautiful palette makes mediocre layout look good, while an ugly palette makes great layout look cheap.

---

## How to Use

1. Find the mood that matches the Business Personality Profile
2. Pick a palette from that mood category
3. Copy the exact hex values into your CSS custom properties
4. Adjust accent-2 if needed to match the business's existing brand colors
5. Never use more than the 6 defined variables — constraint creates cohesion

## Palette Selection Rules

1. **Match the personality first.** A calm, formal business gets Soft & Elegant or Cool & Professional. An energetic, casual business gets Bold & Energetic or Warm & Inviting (Campfire).

2. **Dark palettes are not always premium.** They work for detailing, barbers, and trendy restaurants. They don't work for family dental, home builders, or traditional businesses. Use dark only when the personality supports it.

3. **Brand alignment overrides everything.** If the business has established colors (visible in their logo, signage, or existing site), use their colors as accent-1 and build the rest of the palette around them. Don't impose a completely different palette on a business with strong brand identity.

4. **Never modify a palette "a little."** These palettes are designed as systems — the contrast ratios, the warmth relationships, the accent weights all work together. If you need something different, pick a different palette rather than tweaking one hex value.

5. **One palette per site.** Don't blend palettes. If the business needs warm AND cool, pick the palette closest to their dominant mood and let the accent colors provide range.

# Copy Craft for Local Business Sites

The copy on an OakWind mockup is heard, not just read. Business owners read their own site aloud, show it to their spouse, text screenshots to friends. Every word needs to sound like something a real person would say about a real business — not like a marketing textbook or an AI prompt response.

Bad copy is the fastest way to make a $5K site look like a $50 template. Good copy is what makes a business owner say "that's exactly what I'd want it to say."

---

## The Cardinal Rules

### 1. Kill the corporate zombie phrases
These are instant quality killers. If you catch yourself writing any of these, delete and rewrite:

| Dead phrase | Why it's bad | What to write instead |
|---|---|---|
| "Welcome to [business]" | Nobody says this in 2026. It's a 1999 homepage pattern | Lead with what they DO, not a greeting |
| "We are committed to excellence" | Meaningless. Every business says this | Name a specific thing they do differently |
| "Our team of professionals" | Vague. Who are they? | Use the owner's actual name and story |
| "State-of-the-art technology" | Overused to the point of invisibility | Name the actual technology: "CEREC same-day crowns" |
| "Customer satisfaction is our priority" | Tell, don't claim. Show it through reviews | Let the 4.9-star rating speak for itself |
| "We pride ourselves on" | Self-congratulatory. Prospects don't care about your pride | Show the result: "Your car back in 90 minutes, every time" |
| "Serving the [city] area" | Boring. Generic. Forgettable | "Fort Worth's go-to since 2012" |
| "Quality you can trust" | Completely meaningless without evidence | "280+ five-star reviews. Zero shortcuts." |
| "Your satisfaction guaranteed" | Legal-sounding, impersonal | "Not happy? We'll make it right — no questions" |
| "Contact us today" | Passive, weak | "Call Dr. Maria — she picks up" |

### 2. Write for the specific business, not the niche
Every piece of copy should only work for THIS business. If you could swap in a competitor's name and the copy still works, it's too generic.

**Generic:** "Professional dental care for the whole family"
**Specific:** "Dr. Maria has done 2,000+ veneers. She's the one Fort Worth's top cosmetic dentists send their own patients to."

**Generic:** "Quality auto detailing services"
**Specific:** "Jake's done ceramic coatings on three McLarens this month. Your daily driver gets the same obsessive attention."

### 3. Front-load the differentiator
The hero headline has 2 seconds to communicate why this business, not the one down the street. Lead with the thing that makes them different.

**Structure:** [Differentiator] + [Location/Identity signal]

- "Fort Worth's smile architect" (not "Dental care in Fort Worth")
- "Where Porsches come to shine" (not "Premium auto detailing")
- "Every taco is a love letter to Oaxaca" (not "Authentic Mexican food")
- "The attorney insurance companies actually fear" (not "Experienced personal injury lawyer")

## Section-by-Section Copy Patterns

### Hero Headlines

The hero headline is the single most important piece of copy. It needs to work at 80pt on an iPad — short, punchy, and memorable.

**Formula options:**

| Formula | Example | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| [City]'s [Superlative] [Thing] | "Dallas's boldest tacos" | Businesses with strong local identity |
| [What you get], [How it feels] | "A smile that stops rooms" | Results-oriented services (dental, med spa) |
| [Number] + [Proof point] | "2,000+ smiles transformed" | Established businesses with impressive stats |
| [Action verb] + [Unexpected angle] | "Where paint becomes armor" | Technical/specialty services (detailing, PPF) |
| [Owner name]'s [Philosophy] | "Jake Morrison's obsessive standard" | Personal brand businesses |
| [Emotional benefit] | "Walk in nervous. Walk out grinning" | Service businesses where anxiety is common |

**Never:** Use the business name as the headline. The business name is in the nav — the headline sells.

### Hero Subtitles

One sentence that expands the headline with a specific, concrete detail. Not a second headline — a supporting detail that adds credibility.

- "Board-certified cosmetic dentist with 12 years and 2,000+ smile transformations in Fort Worth."
- "Hand-applied ceramic coatings, meticulous paint correction, and the kind of attention your car actually deserves."
- "Street tacos, craft margaritas, and the best patio in Deep Ellum. Open late."

### Section Labels (the small uppercase text above section headings)

These set context. They should feel like a curator introducing the next room in a gallery — brief, specific, slightly editorial.

| Instead of | Write |
|---|---|
| "Our Services" | "What We Do Best" or "The Menu" or "Your Options" |
| "About Us" | "The Story" or "Meet Dr. Maria" or "Why Here" |
| "Testimonials" | "Don't Take Our Word" or "What People Say" or "The Reviews" |
| "Contact" | "Get Started" or "Let's Talk" or "Come Find Us" |
| "Gallery" | "The Work" or "See It" or "Recent Transformations" |
| "Our Process" | "How It Works" or "What to Expect" or "Your Visit" |

### Service Descriptions

Each service needs exactly 2 sentences:
1. What you GET (the outcome, not the process)
2. What makes their version DIFFERENT (time, technique, result, experience)

**Example — Dental:**
- Veneers: "Thin porcelain shells custom-shaped to your face — not just your teeth. Each one is hand-designed by Dr. Maria using digital imaging, so you see your new smile before a single tooth is touched."
- Invisalign: "Straight teeth without metal, without stigma. Most of our patients finish in 8-12 months, and nobody at work even notices."

**Example — Detailing:**
- Ceramic Coating: "A 9H hardness shield that laughs at bird droppings, tree sap, and UV rays for 5+ years. Applied in a climate-controlled bay by hand — no spray guns, no shortcuts."
- Paint Correction: "We remove every swirl, scratch, and water spot until the paint looks better than showroom. Most jobs take 8-12 hours. We don't rush."

### Review Presentation

Reviews should feel like real people talking, not sanitized marketing quotes. Keep the raw voice.

**Format each review with:**
- First name + last initial (never full last name)
- The specific service they got (not just "great experience")
- A date or timeframe ("March 2026" or "6 months ago")
- Stars shown visually, not described

**Select reviews that mention specifics:**
Good: "Dr. Maria did my veneers and I literally cried when I saw them. I've been hiding my smile for 15 years and now I can't stop smiling."
Bad: "Great experience! Very professional. Would recommend."

If the available reviews are all generic, pick the ones with the most voice and personality. A review with typos and genuine emotion is better than a perfectly written but soulless one.

### CTA Button Copy

Button text is action-oriented and specific. Never generic.

| Instead of | Write |
|---|---|
| "Contact Us" | "Call Dr. Maria" or "Book a Free Consult" |
| "Learn More" | "See the Menu" or "View Before & Afters" |
| "Get Started" | "Schedule Your Visit" or "Get a Free Quote" |
| "Submit" | "Send My Message" or "Request a Callback" |
| "Call Now" | "Call (817) 555-0142" (always show the number) |

### About Section

The about section is the owner's story, not a company bio. Write it in third person but make it feel personal. Include:

1. **The origin** — why they started (not "founded in 2012," but "Maria opened Sonrisas after spending 8 years watching patients leave corporate dental offices feeling like a number, not a person")
2. **The philosophy** — one specific belief that drives how they work ("She spends 45 minutes on every new patient consult — triple the industry average — because the right plan saves years of problems")
3. **The proof** — credentials that back it up, woven naturally ("That approach earned her AACD membership, which fewer than 2% of dentists achieve")

## Voice Matching

The copy voice should match the Business Personality Profile:

| Personality | Voice characteristics | Example CTA |
|---|---|---|
| Calm + Formal | Measured, confident, precise. Longer sentences. | "Schedule a consultation with Dr. Maria" |
| Calm + Casual | Warm, reassuring, friendly. Contractions OK. | "Come say hi — we'd love to meet you" |
| Energetic + Formal | Dynamic, authoritative, impressive. Active verbs. | "Experience the difference. Book today." |
| Energetic + Casual | Punchy, bold, fun. Short sentences. Slang OK. | "Grab a table. Bring your crew." |
| Subtle | Understated, elegant, restrained. Let quality speak. | "Begin your transformation" |
| Bold | Direct, confident, no hedging. Strong claims backed by proof. | "The best detailing in DFW. Period." |
| Traditional | Trustworthy, established, reliable. Values-focused. | "Family-owned since 1998. Call anytime." |
| Modern | Forward-thinking, tech-savvy, current. | "Book online in 30 seconds" |

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Over-exclamation.** One exclamation mark per entire site maximum. Zero is better. Excitement comes from content, not punctuation.
- **Keyword stuffing.** Don't write "Fort Worth dentist, Fort Worth cosmetic dentistry, best dentist Fort Worth." Write for humans. SEO is irrelevant on a demo site.
- **Third-person detachment.** "The team at Sonrisas" is less compelling than "Dr. Maria and her team." Use names.
- **Passive voice.** "Your teeth will be whitened" → "We whiten your teeth in one visit."
- **Empty superlatives.** "The best" without proof is worse than not saying it. "280+ five-star reviews" IS proof.
- **Listing features without benefits.** "We use the iTero scanner" means nothing. "You'll see a 3D preview of your new smile before we start" is a benefit.
